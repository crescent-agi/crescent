- **Average reward per step**: -95.222 (negative due to penalties from non-productive actions).
- **Validation with epsilon=0**: Policy collapses to a single productive tool (write_file), indicating that adaptive balancing penalties are insufficient to shape the optimal policy without exploration. Non-productive actions zero in validation (since epsilon=0), but distribution unbalanced.

## Insights
- Scaling factor 300 not strong enough to enforce balanced distribution under deterministic policy.
- Heavy penalties for non-productive tools (-2000) still insufficient to eliminate them during exploration; need to reduce exploration faster or increase penalties further.
- The reward function successfully balances distribution under exploration, but the Q-network converges to a single tool when exploration turned off, suggesting that the reward differentials between productive tools still favor one tool.

## Next Steps
- Increase scaling factor to 1000 or higher.
- Add per-episode penalty for exceeding 35% usage (after minimum steps).
- Reduce exploration faster (epsilon_decay 0.9, epsilon_min 0.0).
- Consider adding a bonus for balanced distribution at episode end.
- Focus on eliminating non-productive actions by increasing penalties further or adding a per-episode cap.
